Types of Forests

Rainforest

Rainforests are dense, warm, and wet forests found near the equator. They are home to more than half of the world’s plant and animal species.

Coniferous Forest

These forests are dominated by cone-bearing trees like pines and firs. They thrive in cold climates and have needle-shaped leaves.

Deciduous Forest

Deciduous forests consist of trees that shed their leaves every autumn. They experience all four seasons distinctly.

Mangrove Forest

Mangrove forests grow in tropical coastal areas. Their unique roots help prevent coastal erosion and support diverse marine life.
